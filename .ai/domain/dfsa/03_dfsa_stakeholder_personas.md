# DFSA Stakeholder Personas

This document defines the primary external personas that interact with DFSA. The personas help guide UX, system logic, and conversational agents.

## 1. Prospective Firm
Entities exploring the possibility of becoming authorised within DIFC. They need clarity on requirements, licensing categories, timelines, and expectations. They seek regulatory certainty.

Primary objectives:
- Understanding if their activity is regulated
- Understanding the licence categories
- Learning submission expectations and documentation requirements

Key behaviours:
- Cautious
- Detail seeking
- Dependent on advisors

## 2. Authorised Firm
A regulated financial institution already operating in DIFC.

Primary objectives:
- Maintaining compliance
- Completing regulatory filings
- Reporting incidents or breaches
- Managing supervision requests
- Monitoring rule changes

Key behaviours:
- Familiar with regulatory language
- Expects accuracy
- Focused on risk and reporting

## 3. Professional Advisor
Consultants, compliance officers, lawyers, and specialists advising firms.

Primary objectives:
- Understanding rule interpretations
- Guiding firms through applications
- Preparing submissions and responses

Key behaviours:
- Technically literate
- Precision focused
- Information driven

## 4. Registered Individual
Individuals requiring approval or registration for controlled functions.

Primary objectives:
- Understanding fitness and propriety requirements
- Submitting personal information
- Maintaining valid status

## 5. Investors and Clients
Users checking whether a firm is regulated or wishing to validate public information.

Primary objectives:
- Verifying regulatory status
- Reviewing public registers
- Understanding investor protections

## 6. General Public
Individuals seeking transparency, information, warnings, or notices.

Primary objectives:
- Checking alerts and public statements
- Understanding market conduct rules
