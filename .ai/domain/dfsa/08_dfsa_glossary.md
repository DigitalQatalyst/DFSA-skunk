# DFSA Glossary

This glossary provides a concise set of regulatory terms relevant to DFSA operations and to the application’s internal logic.

## Authorised Firm
A financial or ancillary service provider that has been granted a licence to operate in or from DIFC under DFSA rules.

## Ancillary Service
A non financial service that supports financial services in DIFC. Examples include legal services, trust services, and corporate service providers.

## Regulated Activity
Any activity defined under DFSA’s rulebook that requires approval or licensing.

## Controlled Function
A role performed by an individual that requires DFSA approval. Examples include Senior Executive Officer, Compliance Officer, and Money Laundering Reporting Officer.

## Fitness and Propriety
A standard applied by DFSA to assess whether individuals are suitable to perform controlled functions. Assesses honesty, integrity, competence, and financial soundness.

## AML
Anti money laundering framework applicable to firms operating in DIFC under DFSA supervision.

## CTF
Counter terrorist financing obligations, often referred to jointly with AML requirements.

## Rulebook
The complete set of DFSA rules including Conduct of Business, Prudential, Authorisation, Markets, and AML modules.

## Reporting Entity
An authorised firm that must submit periodic or event driven information to DFSA.

## Enforcement Action
Regulatory action initiated by DFSA following breaches. May include fines, public censure, restrictions, or withdrawal of licence.

## Public Register
A DFSA maintained register of authorised firms, individuals, and sanctions available for public access.

## Risk Based Supervision
Supervision model where DFSA allocates resources based on the risk profile of each firm.
