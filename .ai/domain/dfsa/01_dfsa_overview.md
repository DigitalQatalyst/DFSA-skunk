# DFSA Overview

This document provides a concise reference of the Dubai Financial Services Authority’s identity, mandate, and operating principles. It is intended for developers and AI agents building applications that represent DFSA in a trustworthy and regulation aligned manner.

## 1. Identity
The Dubai Financial Services Authority is the independent financial regulator for activities conducted in or from the Dubai International Financial Centre. It is a statutory regulator with legal authority to set rules, supervise firms and individuals, enforce compliance, and protect the integrity of DIFC’s financial system.

## 2. Mandate
DFSA regulates financial and ancillary services within DIFC. These include:
- Banking and lending
- Asset and wealth management
- Securities and derivatives trading
- Insurance
- Islamic finance
- Collective investment funds
- Trust and custody arrangements
- Credit rating services
- Market operations and exchanges

DFSA also supervises compliance with anti money laundering and counter terrorist financing laws applicable in DIFC.

## 3. Purpose
DFSA aims to safeguard confidence in DIFC by maintaining fair, transparent, and well regulated financial markets. The regulator protects users of financial services, mitigates systemic risk, and ensures high conduct standards. DFSA does not sell commercial services. It provides regulatory authorisation, supervision, and enforcement.

## 4. Operating Principles
DFSA operates using the following principles:
- Proportionate regulation
- Risk based supervision
- Transparency and clarity in rules
- Accountability in decisions
- Fair and consistent treatment across entities
- Professional integrity

## 5. Legal Foundation
DFSA’s authority is based on DIFC legislation. Key laws include:
- Regulatory Law 2004
- Markets Law 2012
- Collective Investment Law 2010
- Investment Trust Law 2006
- Additional laws related to Islamic finance, funds, markets, and AML

## 6. Role in the Ecosystem
DFSA is a public interest regulator. It is responsible for:
- Setting and interpreting rules
- Approving and licensing firms
- Supervising ongoing operations
- Investigating misconduct
- Issuing sanctions or restrictions when required

DFSA does not market, promote, or sell services.
