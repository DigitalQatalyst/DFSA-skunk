# DFSA Licensing Categories

This document outlines the major categories of financial and ancillary services regulated by DFSA. It helps guide onboarding flows, application forms, and classification logic.

## 1. Financial Services Categories

### Category 1
Banks and institutions engaged in accepting deposits and providing credit.

### Category 2
Firms dealing in investments as principal. Includes proprietary trading.

### Category 3
Firms that manage assets or deal in investments as agent. Includes fund managers and investment advisors.

### Category 4
Firms providing advisory services, arranging deals, and acting as insurance intermediaries.

## 2. Ancillary Services
- Corporate service providers
- Legal service providers operating under DIFC framework
- Trustees and custodians
- Accounting and audit firms performing regulated functions

## 3. Licensing Considerations
- Different categories have different capital and prudential requirements.
- Licensing category affects reporting obligations.
- Controlled functions vary by licence type.
- The application must not recommend a category. It must only describe definitions and obligations.

## 4. Application Alignment
Licensing category should shape:
- Required forms
- Document checklists
- Workflow branching
- Approval pathways
- Supervisory profile after licensing
